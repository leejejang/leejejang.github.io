About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Minnesota, advised by Prof. Yao-Yi Chiang in the Knowledge Computing Lab. My research focuses on computer vision, natural language processing, and multimodal learning, especially when models operate on spatial and geospatial data.

I work on scene text spotting with pretrained language models, human-centered AI agents for street-view analysis, and multimodal systems for dental diagnostics.

Research

Graduate Research Assistant, Knowledge Computing Lab
University of Minnesota · 2022 – Present · Advisor: Prof. Yao-Yi Chiang
  • Integrate pretrained language models into end-to-end scene text spotters for robust multilingual text understanding.
  • Design vision–language pipelines for tooth-level diagnosis using multi-view smartphone images in collaboration with the School of Dentistry.
  • Develop human-centered AI agents that interpret street-view imagery for neighborhood assessment and spatial perception studies.
  • Collaborate with interdisciplinary teams across geography, dentistry, and geoscience on map digitization and geospatial AI projects.
